History of NYC Accidents

Did you know that New York City has over 150k accidents on average every year. In fact, this number has not significantly decreased in the past 4 years. Each year, city planners and the local government have to make hard tradeoffs to determine where to spend their budget on improving and maintaining the safety of their roads. We believe that by exposing the right data to them, they will be better able to prioritize which intersections they should be focus on.

To explore the historical accident data, click on the dots at the bottom to switch between yearly/monthly/daily views of the data.

Methodology

For our accident data, we collected and parsed over 1M collision records between 2013-2016, mapping each collision to the nearest intersection. These records were obtained from NYC OpenData.

To estimate traffic, we collected and parsed over 20M(??) taxi pick-up and drop-off records between 2013-2016 (??). These records were obtained from www.nyc.gov. We computed the shortest path between each of these pick-up and drop-off locations to approximate traffic. After computing all trips, we aggregated these trips to determine how many times each intersection was passed through to come up with a proxy for traffic flow.

After computing aggregate accident and traffic numbers for each intersection, we then computed a safety score using the following formula:

Formula: Accident / Traffic = Score

Formula: (Score - Min) / (Max - Min) = Normalized Score*

*Normalized scores closer to 1 are considered more dangerous.

Interactive map

We've taken accident data, traffic data, and our computed intersection scores and plotted them on a map to easily identify areas that could benefit the most from improvement. There are 2 layering options: heat map and detailed markers. In the heat map view, warmer colors represent more dangerous intersections. In the detailed view, red color markers represent the most dangerous intersections, blue markers represent moderately dangerous intersections, and green markers represent least dangerous intersections. Click on any individual marker to see more details about that intersection.

Intersection Scores

To provide additional insight, we've created a data table that contains data about each intersection using accident data over the last 4 years. This table is scorted by safety score by default, showing the most dangerous intersections first. However, you can sort by additional dimensions or search for interesting intersections.
